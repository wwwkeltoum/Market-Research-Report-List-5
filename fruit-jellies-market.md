<p><ul><li>High Methoxyl Pectin (HMP)</li><li>Low Methoxyl Pectin (LMP)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The fruit jellies market is primarily categorized into two types based on pectin content: High Methoxyl Pectin (HMP) and Low Methoxyl Pectin (LMP). HMP is commonly used for jellies that require high sugar content for gelling, making it ideal for products with rich flavors. In contrast, LMP gels with low sugar levels or in the presence of calcium, allowing for sugar-reduced options and catering to health-conscious consumers. Both types serve diverse applications in food production and confectionery.</p></p>
