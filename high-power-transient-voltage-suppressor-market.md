<p><p>The High Power Transient Voltage Suppressor (TVS) market is segmented into unipolar and bipolar types based on their voltage clamping characteristics. Unipolar TVS devices are designed to handle voltage spikes in one direction, making them suitable for protecting circuits with a single polarity. In contrast, bipolar TVS devices manage voltage transients in both directions, offering protection for AC signals or circuits with dual polarities. Each type serves distinct applications, depending on the system's voltage behavior and requirements.</p></p>
