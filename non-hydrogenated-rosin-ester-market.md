<p><p>The Non-hydrogenated Rosin Ester market consists primarily of two types: Glycerol Ester and Pentaerythritol Ester. Glycerol Esters are produced by the reaction of rosin with glycerol, offering good compatibility with a variety of resins and enhanced stability. Pentaerythritol Esters, derived from pentaerythritol, provide superior hardness and weather resistance, making them suitable for applications in coatings, adhesives, and inks. Both types serve diverse industries, leveraging their unique properties to improve product performance and sustainability.</p></p>
