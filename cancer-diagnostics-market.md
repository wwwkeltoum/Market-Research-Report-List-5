<p><p>The cancer diagnostics market is divided into two primary types: platform-based and instrument-based. </p><p>Platform-based diagnostics involve comprehensive testing systems that facilitate multiple assays and analyses, such as liquid biopsy or next-generation sequencing, allowing for efficient detection and monitoring of cancer. </p><p>In contrast, instrument-based diagnostics focus on specific devices and technologies, like imaging systems or pathology tools, that assist in diagnosing cancer through targeted examinations or biological sample evaluations. Both types work together to improve early detection and patient outcomes.</p></p>
