<p><p>The lithium ores market can be divided into two primary types: saline lake lithium and mineral lithium. Saline lake lithium is extracted from brine deposits found in salt flats, where lithium-rich brine is evaporated to concentrate the lithium content. In contrast, mineral lithium is sourced from hard rock deposits, such as spodumene, where the lithium is extracted through traditional mining techniques. Both types play a crucial role in meeting the growing demand for lithium in electric vehicle batteries and renewable energy storage.</p></p>
