<p><p>The egg stabilizer market includes various types such as cream of tartar, sugar, and starch. Cream of tartar, a natural byproduct of winemaking, helps stabilize egg whites, enhancing volume and texture in meringues and soufflés. Sugar not only sweetens but also stabilizes whipped egg mixtures, promoting structure in baked goods. Starch, often derived from corn or potatoes, acts as a thickening agent, providing stability to mixtures in pastries and sauces. Each type plays a crucial role in food preparation and baking.</p></p>
