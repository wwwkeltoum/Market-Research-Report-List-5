<p><p>Continuous Renal Replacement Therapy (CRRT) is a specialized dialysis technique used primarily in critically ill patients with acute kidney injury. Unlike traditional intermittent hemodialysis, CRRT allows for slow and continuous filtration of blood, which helps maintain hemodynamic stability by preventing drastic changes in fluid and electrolyte balance. This method is particularly beneficial for patients who are hemodynamically unstable, as it minimizes fluctuations in blood pressure and reduces the risk of complications.</p><p>The CRRT market is experiencing significant growth, driven by the rising prevalence of chronic kidney diseases, an increasing aging population, and higher incidences of critical illnesses requiring renal support.
