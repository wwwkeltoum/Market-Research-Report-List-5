<p><p>The Coronary Vascular Closure Devices (VCDs) market is divided into two main types: invasive and non-invasive. Invasive coronary VCDs are typically used during catheter-based procedures to achieve hemostasis after arterial access, ensuring rapid and secure closure of puncture sites. In contrast, non-invasive coronary VCDs focus on external methods for managing vascular integrity, often utilizing pressure or adhesive technologies. Both types aim to minimize complications, enhance patient recovery, and improve overall procedural success in cardiovascular interventions.</p></p>
