<p><p>The laboratory disposable glove market comprises three primary types: latex gloves, rubber gloves, and plastic gloves. Latex gloves, made from natural rubber latex, offer excellent flexibility and tactile sensitivity, making them ideal for tasks requiring precision. Rubber gloves, often more durable and resistant to chemicals, are suitable for handling hazardous materials. Plastic gloves, typically made from polyethylene, provide a cost-effective option for light tasks and food handling, although they lack the durability and sensitivity of latex and rubber varieties.</p></p>
