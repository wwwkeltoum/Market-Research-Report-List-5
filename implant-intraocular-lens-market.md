<p><strong>The Implant Intraocular Lens Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Non-foldable Lenses</li><li>Foldable Intraocular Lens</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The intraocular lens (IOL) market is primarily divided into two types: non-foldable lenses and foldable lenses. Non-foldable lenses are rigid and typically require larger incisions for implantation, making them less versatile. In contrast, foldable intraocular lenses are made from soft materials that allow them to be folded for minimally invasive implantation through smaller incisions. This feature makes foldable lenses more popular, as they promote quicker recovery times and reduce the risk of complications, catering to a broader range of surgical techniques.</p></p>

<p><p>The Implant Intraocular Lens (IOL) market is primarily segmented into hydrophilic and hydrophobic lenses, each serving specific applications in cataract surgery and vision correction. Hydrophilic IOLs, made from water-attracting materials, offer excellent biocompatibility and are often used for patients with dry eyes. Together, these options enhance surgical outcomes and patient satisfaction in ophthalmology.</p></p>
