<p><p>The nonfat dried milk market is segmented into three types based on the heat treatment during processing: low-heat, medium-heat, and high-heat. Low-heat nonfat dried milk is produced at lower temperatures, preserving its functional properties, making it suitable for baking and confectionery. Medium-heat is processed at moderate temperatures, offering a balance of solubility and flavor, ideal for dairy products. High-heat nonfat dried milk undergoes intense heat treatment, enhancing shelf stability but often compromising solubility, making it suitable for longer-term applications.</p></p>
