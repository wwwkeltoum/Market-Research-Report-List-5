<p><p>Fermentation for alternative protein involves various methods to produce sustainable protein sources. Traditional fermentation utilizes microorganisms to enhance food products, often seen in yogurt or cheese. Biomass fermentation focuses on cultivating microbial cells to create protein-rich biomass, commonly employed for mycoproteins in products like meat substitutes. Precision fermentation leverages engineered microbes to produce specific proteins, such as enzymes or growth factors, enabling the creation of animal-free protein with high purity and efficiency, significantly advancing the alternative protein market.</p></p>
