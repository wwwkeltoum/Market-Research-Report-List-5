<p><p>In the gaming market, sound cards are categorized into external and internal types. External sound cards are standalone devices that connect to a computer via USB or other ports, offering easy portability and enhanced sound quality. They are ideal for gamers who want flexibility and may use multiple devices. Internal sound cards, on the other hand, are installed directly into the computer's motherboard, providing higher performance and lower latency, making them suitable for dedicated gaming rigs seeking superior audio precision.</p></p>
