<p><p>The postpartum depression therapeutics market includes various classes of antidepressants. Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs) are commonly prescribed due to their efficacy and safety profile for new mothers. Serotonin Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors (SNRIs) offer an alternative, addressing both serotonin and norepinephrine pathways. Tricyclic Antidepressants (TCAs) are older medications effective for severe cases, but with more side effects. Monoamine Oxidase Inhibitors (MAOIs) are less frequently used due to dietary restrictions and potential interactions, yet can be effective for resistant cases.</p></p>
