<p><p>Bare Metal Coronary Artery Stents (BM-CAS) are small tube-like devices placed in narrowed or blocked coronary arteries to keep them open and facilitate blood flow. Unlike drug-eluting stents, which release medication over time, bare metal stents are composed solely of metal and serve primarily as scaffolding for the vessel. They are often used in patients with a low risk of restenosis, or re-narrowing of the artery.</p><p>The Bare Metal Coronary Artery Stent Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.8% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to the increasing prevalence of coronary artery diseases, a rise in the aging population, and advancements in stenting technology. Additionally, the ongoing shift towards minimally invasive procedures further boosts market demand. </p><p>Emerging markets are witnessing substantial growth, driven by increasing healthcare investments and greater accessibility to interventional cardiology services.
