<p><p>Electroactive Polymers (EAPs) are materials that exhibit significant mechanical deformation when subjected to an electric field. The market for EAPs is primarily segmented into two types: electronic and ionic. Electronic EAPs utilize electric fields to induce changes in shape or size, making them suitable for applications like actuators and sensors. Ionic EAPs, on the other hand, rely on ion movement within the polymer matrix, allowing for softer, more compliant actions, often used in artificial muscles and soft robotics. Both types are increasingly sought after for advancements in medical devices, consumer electronics, and robotics.</p></p>
